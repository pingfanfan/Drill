# Question Bank - Cognitive Adversary Framework

## Universal Questions (All Domains)

### Layer 2: Unspoken Consensus Extraction

**Core question variants:**

- What does every successful player in this market understand that their customers never say out loud?
- If you interviewed 100 insiders, what would they all agree on that outsiders miss?
- What do customers actually want that they cannot articulate?
- What "common sense" in this space is actually hard-won wisdom?
- What do the winners do consistently that they rarely discuss?

**Probing follow-ups:**

- What behavior pattern contradicts what people say they want?
- What do frustrated customers reveal about the real job-to-be-done?
- What do successful pivots have in common?
- What do failed attempts consistently get wrong?
- What is the 20% that drives 80% of outcomes?

### Layer 3: Assumption Deconstruction

**Core question variants:**

- What 3 assumptions is this market/industry/strategy built upon?
- What would have to be true for this to work spectacularly?
- What does "everyone know" that might not actually be true?
- What beliefs, if wrong, would collapse this entire approach?
- What are the load-bearing walls of this structure?

**Fragility testing:**

- What conditions would cause each assumption to fail?
- Who is already acting as if this assumption is false?
- What edge cases contradict the standard narrative?
- What would a newcomer assume instead?
- What has changed recently that might invalidate old truths?

### Layer 4: Adversarial Challenge

**Investor/destroy mode:**

- Write the 5 questions a top-tier investor would use to destroy this idea.
- If you wanted to short this strategy, what would be your thesis?
- What would make this fail that nobody is talking about?
- Where is the evidence weakest in this plan?
- What would a skeptical board member focus on?

**Competitor/attack mode:**

- How would a smart competitor exploit this strategy's weaknesses?
- What vulnerabilities does this approach create?
- Where is the defensive moat thinnest?
- What would a counter-positioning look like?
- How would you attack this if you were on the other side?

**Customer/skeptic mode:**

- Why should customers trust this over the established alternative?
- What would make customers regret choosing this?
- What are we asking customers to believe that they have no evidence for?
- What friction are we ignoring that customers will feel immediately?

### Layer 5: Quality Question Generation

**Meta-questions:**

- What question am I not asking?
- What would make this decision obvious?
- What would have to be true for the opposite choice to be right?
- What would cause me to change my mind?
- What question does this answer generate?

**Decision-clarifying:**

- What would we do differently if we knew we were right?
- What would we do differently if we knew we were wrong?
- What is the minimum evidence needed to decide?
- What decision does this analysis need to inform?
- What would "good enough" look like?

## Domain-Specific Questions

### Market Analysis

**Layer 2 - Market Structure:**

- What do incumbents know about customer acquisition costs that newcomers underestimate?
- What unspoken rules govern competitive behavior in this space?
- What does "winning" actually look like in this market?
- What do successful entrants do differently than the playbook suggests?

**Layer 3 - Market Assumptions:**

- What is the assumed customer lifecycle in this market?
- What is the assumed unit economics model?
- What is the assumed distribution mechanism?
- What would happen if any of these assumptions broke?

**Layer 4 - Market Challenges:**

- What would make this market suddenly unattractive?
- What external factors could reshape this landscape?
- What customer behavior shift would invalidate this opportunity?
- What regulatory change would be existential?

### Product Strategy

**Layer 2 - User Reality:**

- What do power users know that casual users never discover?
- What job is the user actually hiring this product to do?
- What workaround are users doing that indicates an unmet need?
- What friction do users accept as "just how it is"?

**Layer 3 - Product Assumptions:**

- What is assumed about how users discover this product?
- What is assumed about the switching cost from alternatives?
- What is assumed about user willingness to learn new patterns?
- What is assumed about what users will pay for?

**Layer 4 - Product Challenges:**

- What would make users immediately abandon this?
- What feature omission would be fatal?
- What complexity would kill adoption?
- What competitor move would make this irrelevant?

### Business Model

**Layer 2 - Commercial Reality:**

- What do successful companies know about pricing that isn't obvious?
- What unspoken agreements exist between businesses and customers?
- What does "value" actually mean in this transaction?
- What are customers really paying for vs. what they think they are paying for?

**Layer 3 - Business Assumptions:**

- What is assumed about customer lifetime value?
- What is assumed about acquisition channel sustainability?
- What is assumed about margin structures?
- What is assumed about network effects?

**Layer 4 - Business Challenges:**

- What would compress margins to unprofitability?
- What would make customer acquisition costs unsustainable?
- What would break the unit economics?
- What would make this un-investable?

### Competitive Strategy

**Layer 2 - Competitive Reality:**

- What do market leaders fear that they don't discuss publicly?
- What vulnerabilities do all players share?
- What is the actual basis of competition vs. the stated basis?
- What do successful challengers understand about incumbents?

**Layer 3 - Competition Assumptions:**

- What is assumed about barriers to entry?
- What is assumed about differentiation possibilities?
- What is assumed about customer loyalty?
- What is assumed about response times?

**Layer 4 - Competition Challenges:**

- What would make differentiation impossible?
- What would trigger a price war we cannot win?
- What would make our advantage temporary?
- What would incumbents do if they took us seriously?

### Technology/Engineering

**Layer 2 - Technical Reality:**

- What do experienced engineers know about this stack that newcomers miss?
- What technical debt is considered "acceptable" by veterans?
- What scalability challenges are being deferred?
- What complexity is hidden beneath the abstraction?

**Layer 3 - Technical Assumptions:**

- What is assumed about system reliability?
- What is assumed about team expertise?
- What is assumed about integration complexity?
- What is assumed about maintenance burden?

**Layer 4 - Technical Challenges:**

- What would cause catastrophic failure?
- What would make scaling impossible?
- What security assumption is most dangerous?
- What technical decision would be hardest to reverse?

### Go-to-Market

**Layer 2 - GTM Reality:**

- What do successful GTM leaders know about this buyer that salespeople miss?
- What is the actual decision process vs. the documented one?
- What unspoken criteria influence purchasing?
- What do successful campaigns have in common that is never discussed?

**Layer 3 - GTM Assumptions:**

- What is assumed about buyer urgency?
- What is assumed about sales cycle length?
- What is assumed about champion identification?
- What is assumed about competitive evaluation?

**Layer 4 - GTM Challenges:**

- What would lengthen sales cycles unpredictably?
- What would kill conversion rates?
- What would make customer acquisition unsustainable?
- What competitive response would be devastating?

## Question Sequences by Scenario

### New Market Entry

**Sequence:**
1. What do successful entrants understand about this market that incumbents have forgotten?
2. What 3 assumptions justify the current market structure?
3. Which assumption is most vulnerable to change?
4. What would an incumbent do if they took us seriously?
5. What would make entry impossible after we commit?

### Product Launch

**Sequence:**
1. What do early adopters know about this problem that mainstream users miss?
2. What is assumed about how users will discover this?
3. What would cause immediate rejection at first use?
4. What feature gap would competitors exploit?
5. What would make this a "must-have" vs. "nice-to-have"?

### Investment Decision

**Sequence:**
1. What do successful investors in this space understand that outsiders miss?
2. What 3 assumptions justify this valuation?
3. What would cause each assumption to fail?
4. What would a short seller focus on?
5. What evidence would change my conviction?

### Strategic Pivot

**Sequence:**
1. What do successful pivots have in common that failed pivots miss?
2. What assumptions from the old strategy are we carrying forward?
3. Which legacy assumptions most endanger the new direction?
4. What would prove this pivot was a mistake?
5. What would we need to believe to double down?

### Partnership Evaluation

**Sequence:**
1. What do successful partnerships in this space understand about alignment?
2. What assumptions about incentives underpin this deal?
3. What would cause incentive misalignment?
4. What would make this partnership a competitive disadvantage?
5. What terms would protect against the biggest risks?

## Question Quality Checklist

Before using a question, verify:

- [ ] **Specific**: Not generic ("What could go wrong?" → "What single point of failure would be catastrophic?")
- [ ] **Evidence-grounded**: Can be answered with available materials
- [ ] **Actionable**: Answer would change a decision
- [ ] **Non-obvious**: Not something easily found via search
- [ ] **Falsifiable**: Could be proven wrong
- [ ] **Recursive**: Leads to deeper questions
- [ ] **Structural**: Reveals underlying patterns
- [ ] **Adversarial**: Challenges comfortable assumptions

## Building Custom Questions

### Question Templates

**Structure-based:**
- "What structure produces [observed behavior]?"
- "What would [competitor/customer/regulator] need to believe for [action] to make sense?"
- "What is the load-bearing assumption behind [practice]?"

**Inversion-based:**
- "What would guarantee failure?"
- "What would make the opposite decision correct?"
- "What would prove this assumption false?"

**Temporal-based:**
- "What will be true in 3 years that is not true now?"
- "What was true 5 years ago that is no longer true?"
- "What would accelerate the timeline? What would delay it?"

**Perspective-based:**
- "What does [stakeholder] know that [other stakeholder] misses?"
- "How would [expert in different field] view this?"
- "What would [successful company in adjacent space] do differently?"

### Question Stacking

Build sequences that drill deeper:

```
Level 1: What is happening?
Level 2: Why is it happening?
Level 3: What makes that possible?
Level 4: What would change that?
Level 5: What would we do then?
```

### Question Testing

Before finalizing a custom question, test it:

1. **The Google test**: Can this be answered with a web search? If yes, make it more specific.
2. **The expert test**: Would an expert find this question interesting? If no, dig deeper.
3. **The decision test**: Would answering this change what we do? If no, it is likely irrelevant.
4. **The adversary test**: Would an opponent hope we don't ask this? If no, it is likely too soft.
